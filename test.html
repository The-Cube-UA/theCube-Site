Which printer is best for my project?

For most projects we recommend one of our Dremel printers. They are extremely reliable and are capable of printing at rather low resolutions.

If you need higher detail or extremely small parts, we recomend one of our resin printers. Please note that these are generally reserved for reseach or senior design, but can be made available for students who show a need for the technology.

For larger prints, we recomend our Ultimaker S5 or Dimension 1200. The both the Dimension and S5 feature a large print volume and soluble support material. Please note that the Dimension is very expensive to run and is generally reserved for research or senior design.

More questions? Come by 1003 H.M. Comer or email us.

What filament should I choose?

For most applications, PLA is more than suitable. Please note that if the part will be exposed to heat, or left outside, it could deform over long periods of time.

Alternatively, ABS and Onyx are slightly stronger than PLA and more resilent to heat.

The Cube also maintains small stocks of Carbon Fiber, Kevlar, and other fibers for the MarkForged MK II. Please note that these are extremely expensive materials and are reserved for research and extreme need cases.
